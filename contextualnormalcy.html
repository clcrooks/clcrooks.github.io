Case Study

Contextual Normalcy

Designing an audio-visual experience for emotional release

Description

Contextual Normalcy is a participatory AI research project in which machine learning (ML) and crowd-sourced data are used to create alternative visions of normalcy in feeling, mental health, and wellbeing. In this latest iteration, we turn to sound to explore these questions because of its intimate connections to time, space, and place-making. This iteration is an interactive multimedia experience utilizing user-contributed data to explore the 'sound feeling' across places in a shared time of distance.

Artists
Carey Crooks, Michaella Moon
Location
Los Angeles
Stack
P5.JS, MAX MSP
Role
Designed UI/UX and built visual component for an on-going collaborative project

Contextual Normalcy was exhibited at tender intelligences, an international collaboration between LA-based Mutable Studio and Berlin-based Zentrum für Netzkunst.
